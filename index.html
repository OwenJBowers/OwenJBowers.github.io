Spool West

Last time I drove through Ohio

I was headed East, and

the woman I love still answered.

the phone, and Spaceman was still alive

John too and Prince and now

I head home, alive, driving West

along this long spool of road-

the seam along which this country begins

to unravel-I cut through the night like a bullet

passing back through its exit wound

in hopes of burning closed the flesh.

I see in the distance my friendâ€™s murderer

and his mother at the same time, it is-

as it has always been-unfathomable to me

that they are the same person.

Perhaps each knife wound after

the first was a frantic attempt to undo

what had been done. I press the gas harder,

the truck straining under what it cannot bear

to do, the needle of the speedometer flickers

as I stare into the shapeless fields.
